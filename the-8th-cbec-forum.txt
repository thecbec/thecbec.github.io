The 8th International Academic Forum on Cross-Border E-Commerce Held in Kunming, China

The 8th International Academic Forum on Cross-Border E-Commerce was held in Kunming, Yunnan Province, from September 20 to 21, 2025. The forum was jointly organized by Huazhong Agricultural University and Yunnan Agricultural University under the theme “Digital Empowerment and Global Connectivity: Cross-Border E-Commerce and High-Quality Agricultural Development.”

Professor Yan Tingwu, Vice Dean of the College of Economics and Management at Huazhong Agricultural University, attended the forum alongside Professors Zhou Deyi, Xia Chunping, and Xiong Xueping, as well as Associate Professors Li Fan and Liu Xiaotian. Members of the delegation delivered presentations and took part in academic discussions with scholars and industry representatives from China and abroad. The forum provided a venue for exchanging views on current research and practice related to the integration of cross-border e-commerce and agriculture, with particular attention to agricultural digital transformation and international trade cooperation.

The opening ceremony featured remarks by Li Hong, Vice President of Yunnan Agricultural University; Chen Rui, Dean of its College of Economics and Management; and Yan Tingwu. Speakers highlighted the growing role of cross-border e-commerce within the global digital economy and its influence on international trade and agricultural development. Professor Yan emphasized that cross-border e-commerce extends beyond commercial activity, serving as a broader driver of social and economic change. He noted that the pursuit of high-quality agricultural development is closely linked to the expansion of cross-border e-commerce, while the sector’s long-term growth also depends on a resilient agricultural base.

Six experts from China and overseas delivered keynote addresses. The speakers included Associate Professor Paul Ingebleek of Wageningen University in the Netherlands; Carlotta Clivio of the International Training Centre of the International Labour Organization; Lu Chunyang, Executive Deputy Director of the National Steering Committee for Vocational Education in E-Commerce; Associate Professor Akkhapon Kokkhangplu of Khon Kaen University; Associate Professor Danaitun Pongpatcharatorntep of Chiang Mai University; and Dr. Lim Ai Fen of UCSI University, Malaysia. Their presentations covered topics such as market system analysis, value creation and technology adoption in agricultural cross-border e-commerce, regional economic cooperation, and talent development.

The forum included three parallel sessions focusing on agricultural digital transformation, innovation in cross-border e-commerce supply chains, and education and training for cross-border e-commerce professionals.

Representatives from government, industry, and academia also participated in industry briefings and roundtable discussions. Li Daikai, Deputy Director of the Department of Commerce of Yunnan Province, reviewed the current state of cross-border e-commerce in Yunnan, discussed key challenges and opportunities, and outlined relevant policy support measures.

The forum strengthened academic and professional exchange on cross-border e-commerce and high-quality agricultural development and reinforced international dialogue in this field. Participants shared perspectives, identified common priorities, and outlined directions for future collaboration, with an emphasis on expanding international cooperation and advancing integrated development between cross-border e-commerce and agriculture.
